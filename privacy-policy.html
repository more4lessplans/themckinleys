01

Who We Are & Scope

The landlord or property manager of 12 Dugdale Avenue, Beeton, Ontario (referred to throughout as "we," "us," or "the Landlord") is responsible for the personal information collected through this rental application.

This policy applies solely to information collected via the online rental application form associated with this property. It does not apply to any third-party websites or services that may be linked from this application.

02

Information We Collect

We collect only the personal information that is reasonably necessary to evaluate your tenancy application. This may include:

  • Identity information — full legal name, date of birth, government-issued identification type (e.g. driver's licence, passport)
  • Contact information — email address, phone number
  • Residential history — current and previous addresses, length of stay, landlord references
  • Employment & income information — employer name, job title, employment status, gross annual income, supporting documents such as pay stubs or T4s
  • Financial documents — recent bank statements or proof of income, uploaded voluntarily by the applicant
  • Occupancy information — number of occupants, pets, vehicles
  • Co-applicant information — the same categories listed above, for any co-applicant added to the form
  • References — names and phone numbers of personal or professional references
  • Electronic signature — typed name and date, constituting acknowledgement of consents on the application

We do not collect Social Insurance Numbers (SIN), full credit card numbers, or any biometric information through this form.

03

Purpose of Collection

Under PIPEDA Principle 3 (Identifying Purposes), we are required to identify the purpose for which we collect personal information before or at the time of collection. Information collected through this application is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • To assess your suitability as a tenant for the residential unit
  • To verify your identity, employment, and income
  • To conduct credit checks via Equifax or TransUnion, with your explicit consent
  • To contact your references to verify your tenancy history and character
  • To communicate with you regarding the outcome of your application
  • To prepare a lease agreement should your application be accepted

We will not use your information for any purpose beyond these without obtaining your additional consent.

04

Legal Basis & Consent

The legal basis for collecting and processing your personal information is your express, informed consent, given at the time of application submission via the consent checkboxes on the application form.

Under PIPEDA, consent must be meaningful. You have the right to withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice. Withdrawal of consent may mean we are unable to process or continue reviewing your application.

To withdraw consent, please contact us using the information in Section 13 of this policy.

05

How We Use Your Information

Your information will be used only for the purposes stated in Section 3. Specifically:

  • Application submissions are received and stored through FormBold, a third-party secure form-processing service (see Section 12)
  • Information is reviewed only by the Landlord or their authorized property manager
  • Uploaded documents are used solely to verify the information provided in the form
  • Reference contacts will be called or emailed to verify the information you have provided
  • If your application is successful, relevant information will be incorporated into a written lease agreement under the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

06

Disclosure to Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party. Disclosure is limited strictly to the following circumstances:

  • Credit bureaus (Equifax Canada and/or TransUnion Canada) — solely to conduct a credit check, with your prior written consent
  • FormBold — our form processing service, which transmits and stores your submission data on our behalf (see Section 12)
  • Legal obligation — if required by law, court order, or a lawful request from a government authority
  • Property management — if a licensed property manager acts on behalf of the Landlord in evaluating applications, they are bound by equivalent privacy obligations

In all cases of disclosure, we take steps to ensure that third parties provide a comparable level of protection for your personal information.

07

Credit & Background Checks

With your explicit consent (obtained via the consent checkbox on the application), we may obtain a consumer credit report from Equifax Canada or TransUnion Canada. This is a soft inquiry for the purpose of tenant screening and does not affect your credit score.

We do not conduct criminal background checks as part of this application process. Under the Ontario Human Rights Code, making tenancy decisions based on a criminal record unrelated to the tenancy would constitute prohibited discrimination.

Credit information obtained will be used solely to assess financial suitability and will not be retained beyond the period necessary for the application decision.

08

Retention & Destruction

Under PIPEDA Principle 5 (Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention), personal information must only be retained as long as necessary to fulfil the identified purposes.

  • Unsuccessful applications — information is securely and permanently deleted within 90 days of the application decision being communicated to you
  • Successful applications — information necessary for the lease and tenancy management will be retained for the duration of the tenancy and for a reasonable period thereafter (up to 7 years), as required by applicable law and accounting obligations
  • Uploaded documents — income and ID documents are deleted as soon as the application decision is made, unless required for lease execution

Destruction is carried out by permanent deletion from all systems and secure shredding of any printed copies.

09

Security Safeguards

We implement security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the information, consistent with PIPEDA Principle 7 (Safeguards). These include:

  • TLS encryption in transit — all data submitted through the form is transmitted over HTTPS/TLS
  • Access controls — only the Landlord and authorized property manager have access to submitted application data
  • Third-party security — FormBold maintains its own security practices, including encrypted storage and access controls (see Section 12)
  • No physical distribution — applications are not printed or shared outside of the digital review process unless strictly necessary

In the event of a breach of security safeguards that creates a real risk of significant harm to you, we will notify you and the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) as required under PIPEDA's breach reporting obligations.

10

Your Rights Under PIPEDA

As an individual whose personal information we hold, you have the following rights under PIPEDA. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us (see Section 13).

Right of Access
You may request access to the personal information we hold about you and receive an account of how it has been used and disclosed. We will respond within 30 days of receiving your request.
Right to Correction
If the personal information we hold is inaccurate or incomplete, you may request that we amend it. We will take reasonable steps to correct the information and, where appropriate, notify third parties to whom it was disclosed.
Right to Withdraw Consent
You may withdraw your consent to the collection, use, or disclosure of your information at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice. Withdrawal may limit our ability to process your application.
Right to Complain
You have the right to challenge our compliance with PIPEDA and file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) at priv.gc.ca or 1-800-282-1376.
Right to Explanation
If your application is declined, you may request an explanation of the general factors that led to the decision, to the extent we are able to provide one consistent with the privacy interests of others.

11

Ontario Human Rights Protections

The Ontario Human Rights Code prohibits landlords from discriminating against applicants on the basis of any of the following protected grounds:

  • Race, colour, ancestry, place of origin, or ethnic origin
  • Citizenship or immigration status
  • Creed (religion)
  • Sex, gender identity, or gender expression
  • Sexual orientation
  • Age (18 years or older)
  • Marital or family status
  • Disability
  • Receipt of public assistance (source of income)
  • Record of offences for which a pardon has been granted

Tenancy decisions are made solely on the basis of financial qualification, rental history, and reference verification. No information collected through this form is used, and no questions are asked, for purposes that would discriminate on any of the above grounds.

If you believe you have been discriminated against in connection with this application, you may file a complaint with the Human Rights Tribunal of Ontario at hrto.ca or contact the Human Rights Legal Support Centre at 1-866-625-5179.

Cookies & Form Processors

The application page does not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics scripts. The only external resource loaded is the Google Fonts API, which retrieves font files. Google may log this request in accordance with its own privacy policy, which does not include personal data from your application.

FormBold's own privacy policy applies to the handling of data on their servers and can be reviewed at formbold.com/privacy.
